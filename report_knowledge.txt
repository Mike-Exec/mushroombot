MUSHROOMS' TWO CUSTOMERS - EXECULYTICS CONSULTING - MARCH 2026
Research conducted for Windmill Farms
Total survey responses: 1,027

=== SURVEY DEMOGRAPHICS ===
Gender: 55% Female, 45% Male
Age: 25% aged 20-34, 27% aged 35-49, 28% aged 50-64, 20% aged 65+
Region: 58% Ontario, 32% Quebec, 10% Atlantic Canada
Income: 34% under $50K, 43% $50-$99K, 13% $100-$150K, 9% $150K+

=== EXECUTIVE SUMMARY ===
The report identifies two distinct consumer mindsets:
1. The "Old Guard" - large, reliable base who see mushrooms as a staple. Buy white, cremini, portobello. Prioritize price and quality. Sautee or add to soups/stews/sauces. Mushrooms are dependable but rarely exciting.
2. The "New Guard" - smaller but influential, often younger. Motivated by curiosity, variety, experience. More open to organic and exotic varieties. Most memorable experiences in restaurants, not at home.

Key insight: Two-thirds of shoppers are actively looking for inspiration. Consumption is not constrained by availability but by imagination and capability.

=== HOW DO YOU PREPARE MUSHROOMS AT HOME ===
- Sauteed: 83% (dominant method)
- Soups/Stews/Sauces: 52%
- Raw: 31%
- Oven Roasted: 26%
- Grilled/BBQ: 25%
- Baked or Stuffed: 20%

Demographic notes:
- Younger cooks aged 20-34 more likely to grill/BBQ mushrooms
- Younger cooks also lean toward baked preparations
- Higher income households ($150K+) show stronger interest in oven cooked mushrooms

=== GO-TO COOKING METHOD (consumer voice) ===
The most common method is sauteing in butter or olive oil over medium-high heat with garlic, onions, salt and pepper. Second most common is adding to stir-fries, sauces, and stews. Mushrooms are seen as versatile, easy, and fitting naturally into everyday cooking.

=== WHAT WOULD IMPROVE ENJOYMENT ===
- Trying new recipes: 64%
- Learning new cooking techniques: 34%
- Neither: 16%

Demographic notes:
- Younger shoppers aged 20-34 more likely to want to learn new cooking techniques
- Households earning $100K-$150K show stronger interest in trying new recipes
- Older shoppers aged 65+ more likely to say NOTHING would change their liking

=== MOST MEMORABLE MUSHROOM EXPERIENCE ===
Most commonly cited: Stuffed mushrooms at a restaurant. Filled with cream cheese, garlic, onion and seasoning, baked together. The mushroom was the star, not a side. Felt indulgent and special. The experience changed how people think about mushrooms - when handled well they can elevate a dish and leave a lasting impression.

=== THREE CORE CONSUMER GROUPS ===

THE CULINARY EXPLORER
- Demographics: Ages 20-34, income over $150K
- Buys weekly or multiple times per week
- High index on organic
- High index on Oyster and Shiitake mushrooms
- Actively uses mushrooms as a meat replacement
- Interested in learning new cooking techniques

THE TRADITIONALIST
- Demographics: Ages 65+
- Buys white whole and cremini
- Highly price-sensitive
- Highly habitual - reports nothing will change their habits
- Unsure about organic labels

THE VALUE SEEKER
- Demographics: Income under $50K
- Less frequent buyer (monthly or less)
- High index on conventional mushrooms
- High index on packaged mushrooms
- Views mushrooms as an occasional add-on rather than a staple
- Driven primarily by price

=== TOP 10 KEY TAKEAWAYS ===
1. Mushrooms are a reliable staple with room to grow. 55% purchased in past year. Clear headroom to drive more routine use.
2. Classic varieties still dominate. White, cremini, portobello lead. Most shoppers move easily between organic and conventional. Younger consumers want organic and exotic.
3. Price (69%) and quality (61%) drive decisions. Branding and organic claims play much smaller roles.
4. Three distinct shopper mindsets: The Culinary Explorer, The Traditionalist, The Value Seeker.
5. Satisfaction is high but value is the gap. Price satisfaction lags all other metrics.
6. Sauteing leads at home but restaurants create the magic. Most memorable experiences happen in restaurants where presentation and technique elevate mushrooms.
7. Inspiration needed. Two-thirds want more recipe ideas. A significant group wants more basic confidence in how to cook mushrooms well.
8. Mushrooms mostly complement meals but growing potential to replace proteins especially among younger shoppers.
9. Taste drives appeal but variety creates curiosity. Exotic varieties feel less approachable - consumers prefer trying them in restaurants first.
10. Dislike is the biggest barrier for non-buyers. Sampling and fresh ideas can help reshape perceptions.

=== STRATEGIC RECOMMENDATIONS ===
For producers:
- Protect and reinforce the core with consistent quality and strong value on staple varieties
- Unlock growth by helping consumers trade up in experience not just product
- Expand assortments with more distinctive varieties
- Invest in education and inspiration
- Bring restaurant-quality ideas into the home through packaging, partnerships, and content
- Connect everyday use with elevated experiences to grow the entire category
